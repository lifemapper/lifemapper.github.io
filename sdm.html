Species Distribution Modeling (SDM) is also known by several other names, including ecological-niche modeling, and habitat modeling. The purpose is to correlate environmental values, for example climate, elevation, soils, and other variables, with geographic locations where a species has been found. This correlation is computed with an "algorithm", to create a model that defines the relationship of the environmental variables to known locations of the species. The model may then be applied to the same types of environmental, possibly for other regions or for predicted past or future climates, to predict whether the species may live in those locations, or under those past or future conditions.
